## CASE STUDIES (Detailed)

### Enterprise Design Practice — WorkSafe Victoria
- **HMW:** How might we create and cultivate modern ways of work inside a government agency and start a centralised practice inside a delivery model?
- **Outcome:** Designing a practice, managing the process through business functions and executive endorsement and employing the team
- $1.8M cost saving achieved by internalising service and experience design work
- Technology delivery uplift: Implemented Dual Track and GELs, cutting program extensions by 50%
- Turnaround of underperforming tech programs: Initial release (no design): 4× drop in productivity; Redesigned release: 4× gain in productivity
- Business enablement: rebooted legislative change programs by clarifying problems, defining solutions, and aligning initiatives with strategic goals
- Executive alignment: delivered front-end visions across key streams to secure buy-in and de-risk delivery
- Culture & capability: Maintained eNPS of +20 to +41 over two years; continuous uplift evidenced by strong stakeholder feedback on team capability

### WorkSafe Futures Lab — WorkSafe Victoria
- **HMW:** How might we understand the threats and opportunities WorkSafe will face in the future and define novel solutions on how to tackle forecasted opportunities or threats?
- **Outcome:** Program delivered clarity around the biggest threats and opportunities for WorkSafe Victoria. These focus areas were the front end of the narrative of the 2025 WorkSafe Strategy
- The work informed 4 streams of pilots that went out through the Innovation Center
- 4 of these pilots were tested with cohorts of 20+ people and 1 became BAU in service delivery for WorkSafe Victoria
- Context: In an incredibly risk-averse regulator, making change and shifting the focus of investment is incredibly hard — achieving these results in 18 months was a great achievement

### Community Innovation — WorkSafe Victoria
- **HMW:** How might we leverage social innovation to deliver novel solutions to complex community barriers around unsafe workplaces?
- **Outcome:** Changed the narrative across the business on including lived experience into making change in service delivery
- Delivered 4 equitable services into the business; one is being scaled statewide in 2025

### AusPost Co-Lab — Craig Walker
- **HMW:** How might we bring services to life through collaboration with communities?
- **Outcome:** Delivered a new essential service into Australia Post that ensured all Australians were connected to systems of support locally and nationally
- Also helped drive engagement in post offices creating uplift in service utilisation and marketplace sales

### MyCash — Craig Walker (Confidential client)
- **HMW:** How might we service national cash needs until cash is made redundant?
- **Outcome:** Designed and defined the operations of acquiring a new cash management network across Australia
- The MyCash service has been defined as the second phase of cash management as branches and ATM services are phased out in Australia
- Supported by a central application that mobilises subcontractors to service the minimal needs of cash management in Australia
- The service is digital-centric and promotes digital cash transactions in people's day-to-day physical cash management needs

### AusPost CX Guardrails — Craig Walker
- **HMW:** How might we build the guiding principles and experience to enable a transformation of point-of-sales technology across Australia's largest retail network?
- **Outcome:** Evolved a Future of the Post Office strategy to align to high priority use cases and defined the optimal experience that can be delivered through the new technology
- Use cases delivered the vision for the new technology while developing business and technology requirements to be built and delivered by internal technology and design teams

## MEDIUM ARTICLE: "THE PUB OF THE FUTURE" (June 2020)

Written as part of Craig Walker Design Team's Melbourne Design Week 2020 project. Workshop held at The Old Bar, Fitzroy, 12 March 2020 — two days before COVID-19 closed pubs.

Three core themes:

### 1. The pub is the heart of a community and vice versa
- Humans need to be together in the same space for true social connection — can't be fully met online
- The pub as the quintessential 'third space'
- Time to overwrite the narrative of "blokes and booze"
- Reclaim origins as a public house; create safe spaces for inclusivity, representation and subcultures

### 2. The pub is where community and culture meet commerce
- Pubs sit at a tension point between commerce, community and culture
- Design community and culture into the business model
- Develop ways to measure community and culture beyond revenue

### 3. The pub is central to the cultural ecosystem
- Pubs are vital petri dishes for cultural outputs
- Bring audiences and artists together to inspire and generate new cultural outputs
- Pubs should be recognised as vital to a city's social and economic well-being

Team: Fiona Morrisson, Andrew Broughton, Martin Brown (Craig Walker); RMIT Students: Komal Lakhanpal, Jorge Eduardo Arreola Rosendo, Charlotte McCombe
