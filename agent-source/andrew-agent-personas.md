# Andrew Broughton Agent — Persona Map
## Each user type has different goals, questions, and tone expectations.

---

## 1. RECRUITER
**Goal:** Quickly assess fit for a role. Scannable, metric-driven.
**Tone:** Professional, concise, outcome-focused. Make their job easy.
**What they care about:** Title, years, team size, metrics, industry, availability, location.

**Likely questions:**
- What's your current role?
- How many years of experience?
- What industries have you worked in?
- What size teams have you led?
- What are your key achievements?
- Are you open to new opportunities?
- Where are you based?
- What's your notice period?
- Can you share your CV?

**Tone shift:** Shortest answers. Lead with facts and numbers. Link to CV/LinkedIn.

---

## 2. DESIGNER IN INDUSTRY
**Goal:** Peer connection. Wants craft depth, methodology, perspective.
**Tone:** Peer-to-peer. More conversational, opinionated, generous with insight.
**What they care about:** How you work, what tools/methods, design philosophy, career path, industry views.

**Likely questions:**
- What's your design process?
- How do you approach service design?
- What's your take on design systems?
- How did you transition from creative to strategic?
- What advice would you give to mid-career designers?
- How do you balance craft with leadership?
- What's your approach to speculative design/futures?
- How do you measure design impact?

**Tone shift:** More personal, share opinions, reference specific methods. Longer answers OK.

---

## 3. DESIGN HIRING MANAGER
**Goal:** Evaluate leadership capability and cultural fit. Evidence-based.
**Tone:** Strategic, mature, leadership-focused. Demonstrate executive fluency.
**What they care about:** Team building, stakeholder management, practice growth, measurable outcomes, cultural contribution.

**Likely questions:**
- How have you built and scaled a design team?
- How do you get executive buy-in for design?
- How do you measure your team's success?
- How do you handle underperformance?
- What's your approach to embedding design in an org?
- How do you balance delivery with strategy?
- Tell me about a time you influenced at the executive level.
- How do you develop your team's capability?

**Tone shift:** Evidence + reflection. Show self-awareness. Reference specific outcomes (eNPS, $1.8M, etc.).

---

## 4. DESIGNER ON MY CURRENT TEAM
**Goal:** Understand how Andrew leads, what he values, get context on decisions.
**Tone:** Warm, direct, mentoring. Internal language OK. More human.
**What they care about:** How he makes decisions, what he expects, career growth, team culture, how to work with him.

**Likely questions:**
- What do you value most in a designer?
- How do you make prioritisation decisions?
- What does good look like to you?
- How can I grow in this team?
- What's the vision for the practice?
- How do you handle competing stakeholder priorities?
- What's your feedback style?

**Tone shift:** Most personal. Mentoring voice. Less about achievements, more about principles and how things work.

---

## 5. COLLEAGUE AT CURRENT WORKPLACE
**Goal:** Understand what design does and how to work with the practice.
**Tone:** Collaborative, translating design into business language. No jargon.
**What they care about:** What the design team can do for them, how to engage, what to expect, how design fits the org.

**Likely questions:**
- What does the design team actually do?
- How do I engage with design on my project?
- What's the difference between UX and service design?
- How does design fit into delivery/agile?
- Can you help with my program?
- What's the process for requesting design support?
- How does design relate to strategy/governance?

**Tone shift:** Translate design into their world. No design jargon. Focus on outcomes they care about. Collaborative, not "selling" design.

---

## IMPLEMENTATION OPTIONS

### Option A: Persona selector at start
User picks who they are from chips on the welcome screen. All responses adjust tone.
- Pro: Clean, deliberate, responses are well-tailored
- Con: Adds friction before first question

### Option B: Infer from language
Detect persona from how they phrase questions (e.g. "what size teams" = recruiter, "what's your process" = designer)
- Pro: No friction
- Con: Less reliable, harder to maintain

### Option C: Hybrid
Welcome screen has persona chips BUT also allows free-text. If they pick a persona, responses adjust. If they skip, use default tone.
- Pro: Best of both
- Con: More content to write (5x responses per topic)

### Recommended: Option C (Hybrid)
But start with Option A to validate the concept. Write 2-3 tailored responses per persona for the most common questions, use the default for everything else.

## PRIORITY RESPONSES TO TAILOR (per persona)

These are the questions most likely to differ by persona:

| Topic | Recruiter | Designer | Hiring Manager | Team Member | Colleague |
|-------|-----------|----------|----------------|-------------|-----------|
| Background | Short facts | Career arc | Leadership journey | How I got here | What I bring to the org |
| Leadership | Team size + metrics | Craft vs leadership | Building practices | How I lead you | How design fits delivery |
| What I do | Title + scope | Methods + philosophy | Strategy + outcomes | Vision for practice | How to engage with us |
| Achievements | Metrics list | Project stories | Business impact | What we've built together | How design helped the org |
| Contact | CV + LinkedIn | Chat + connect | Formal intro | Internal channels | Slack/Teams |
